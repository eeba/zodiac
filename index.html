About Chinese Zodiac

The Chinese zodiac, also known as Shengxiao, is a 12-year cycle in which each year is associated with a specific animal. This ancient system dates back over 2,000 years and is deeply rooted in Chinese culture and astrology.

The Legend of the Zodiac

According to legend, the Jade Emperor held a race to determine which animals would represent the years. The clever Rat finished first by hitching a ride on the Ox, followed by the Ox, Tiger, Rabbit, Dragon, Snake, Horse, Goat, Monkey, Rooster, Dog, and finally the Pig.

How It Works

Your Chinese zodiac sign is determined by your birth year according to the lunar calendar. Each animal sign is believed to influence personality traits, compatibility with others, and even fortune.

The 12 Signs

The 12 animals in order are: Rat, Ox, Tiger, Rabbit, Dragon, Snake, Horse, Goat, Monkey, Rooster, Dog, and Pig. Each sign has unique characteristics and traits that are believed to define people born in that year.
